What is Minecraft?

Minecraft is a "sandbox-building" video game written by Indie developer Markus "Notch" Persson. The game is primarily focused on player creativity and building, allowing them to constuct almost anything out of blocks they gather in the game world (think virtual Lego bricks). The game also features a "survival mode" where, in addition to constructing objects, the player must also gather resources to maintain their well-being.

Markus Persson begin development of Minecraft in May of 2009 and released the first alpha build soon after. In September 2010, he founded his own video game company, Mojang, with money earned from sales of Minecraft. The first official beta build was released a few months after. After spending roughly a year in beta development, the game was officially released on November 18th, 2011.

What is the "Pocket Edition"?

The Pocket Edition is a scaled-down version of Minecraft intended for portable devices such as smart phones. Originally an exculsive release for the Xperia Play, the Pocket Edition is available for both Android and iOS devices. The Pocket Edition lacks certain features from its desktop counterpart due to limited processing power and having to use a touch screen as a control scheme.

What do I need to play it?

To play Minecraft you need:

  • A fairly recent computer with Java installed
  • An Android phone or tablet with Android 2.2 or greater installed
  • An iPhone or iPad running the most recent version of iOS

Where can I find Minecraft?

Minecraft can be found on its offical webpage, where you have the option of purchasing it for your computer for $26.95. The Android version can be found on the Android Marketplace, where you can also find a trial version, and the iOS version can be found at Apple's App Store. Both the Android and iOS versions cost $6.99. There is also a Lite version for iOS that is free but extremely limited in functionality.
