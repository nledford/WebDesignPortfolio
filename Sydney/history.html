Brief Facts About Sydney

Sydney is the most populous city in Australia

The city's approximate population is 4.6 million

Sydney is the largest city in Australia in land size

The city is 1,687 square kilometers across – roughly 651 square miles

Port Jackon (Sydney Habour) is the largest natural harbour in the world

Sydney was founded in 1788 by Arthur Phillip at Sydney Cove

Sydney was declared the first official city in Australia on July 20, 1842

Sydney is a recurring host of major international sporting events.

Events have included the 1938 British Empire Games, 2000 Summer Olympics, and the final match of the 2003 Rugby World Cup

The $10 note is known as a "smurf"

The note features Andrew Barton Paterson and Mary Gilmore

Designed by Nathaniel Ledford
